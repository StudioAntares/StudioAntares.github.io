QUARTET v4.0

Theatrical Stage Design

The set design for the performance of QUARTET v 4.0 is a site specific installation designed for the unique sci-fi adaptation of dissident playwright Heiner Muller's 1981 original Quartet. An elevated platform floating over the auditorium seating and wrapped in a translucent plastic cube is the centerpiece encapsulating the action of the play. Interactive cameras follow the actors and broadcast real-time video images, which are processed and projected onto screens outside the cube. Lights on the floor and on the cube's edges dynamically interact with the actorsâ€™ movement.

The design process was the epitome of a virtual-office context, coordinating the work of visual artists scattered across the globe - from New York and California to Europe. The project applies simple, inexpensive, off-the-shelf materials to create a beautiful, sophisticated, and elegant environment.

QUARTET v4.0
Produced by Wax Factory at Abrons Art Center, NYC
